Camel

Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  • Even though they appear very slow and laid back, camels are social creatures


Chameleon

Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long
  • Chameleons have good eyesight for a lizard
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues


Cicada

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil
  • All cicada species in North America came from a common ancestor
  • Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source


Panda

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • Although pandas are 99% vegetarian their digestive system is typical of a carnivore. For the 1% of their diet that isn't bamboo, pandas eat eggs, small animals, carrion, and forage in farmland for pumpkin, kidney beans, wheat and domestic pig food
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day


Pigeon

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. They are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition. They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.


Teddy Bear

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall
